First Ricing

2015-11-22 11:00 pm

It’s been a while, and since I have something to share I thought this might be a good time to pick up this blogging business again.

I have started with Linux ricing, i.e. doing “unecessary modifications” to my Linux system. I’ve only been at it for little more than a day, but I must say that I quite enjoy it. There’s a long way to go before I get to one of the increadible ricings displayed over at r/unixporn, which is a subreddit devoted to UNIX ricing. But I would say that my current rice is at least a decent start.

I will keep up the good work, and hope to one day reach a wizardly look on my Debian system!

If anyone is interested in trying my rice out, or have a look at the dotfiles, they’re all available here.

Pump Up the SWAG

2015-05-31 12:00 am

They are here. The long wait has been ended. After a couple of months, I now sit here as a proud owner of Macetech’s brilliant invention the RGB-shades. The shades is a piece of wearable electronics which consists of a controller and an LED-matrix in the shape of a pair of shutter shades. They use the same microcontroller as an Arduino Pro, and can as such be programmed like one.

I have not spent too much time programming them so far, but I can tell you that they are awesome. I do not regret buying this one second, and I will enjoy hacking them. I plan on building a small circuit to make them follow the beat of the music at least, and hopefully I can come up with some other cool extension to them as well.

Meanwhile, enjoy my small “demo” of the patterns I’ve programmed so far.

Happy Hacking!

Real Tools Are Costly

2015-05-19 12:00 am

So, I’ve gotten my “metal files” from China now. I tried filing down one of the metal brush hairs I’ve found which I want to use for material when making lockpicks. However, the files were rubbish. Sure, they are technically metal files as they are made from metal, but they are not build to work on metal. When I tried shaping a pick for myself the file I was trying out was filed down itself. I am a bit dissappointed, but I didn’t really expect anything else considering the files are from China and cost about 1â‚¬ for 10pc. The moral of the story is that good tools are expensive, and that you should not order them from eBay but buy them from a hardware store. Oh well, it was worth a shot, better luck next time etc.

Spring Is Lockpicking My Door

2015-04-29 12:00 am

Spring is here, and for a beginner lockpicker this is great news! Why is it so? Because the streets are being cleaned. That means that machines with large with large steel (spring steel even, I believe) brushes are roaming the streets. This is good because all of a sudden the streets aren’t littered with just grovel, twigs and dust, they are also littered with (spring) steel brush “hairs” which happen to be the perfect size to make your own lockpicking tools. Unfortunately, I’ve only found three, but I will keep my eyes open.

The plan now is to get myself some metal files. I’ve found really cheap ones at eBay, the Lord and Saviour, and I plan to buy these. As they’re shipped from China it might take a while for them to come to Sweden, but I guess I’ll have to be patient. I plan to clean up the “hairs” while I wait, and also try making a tension tool, which really just requires a vice (which I already own).

So that’s pretty much it for now. I’ll write another post when I have made the tension tool and tried that out. Until then!

Happy Hacking!
